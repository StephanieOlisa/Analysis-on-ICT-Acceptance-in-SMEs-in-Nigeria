### Project Overview
This project investigates the acceptance of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) among small and medium enterprises (SMEs), 
focusing on their ICT needs, business challenges, and the degree of ICT adoption. 
Data was gathered via a structured questionnaire, capturing insights from SME representatives on their specific business requirements and challenges related to ICT. 
The data was cleaned using Microsoft Excel's Power Query to ensure accuracy and consistency, followed by preliminary analysis and visualization in Excel. 
Key findings were then presented in a dynamic Power BI dashboard and a Microsoft PowerPoint presentation for stakeholders. 
The project's ultimate aim is to empower SMEs to enhance ICT integration within their operations, addressing adoption barriers and highlighting practical steps for digital transformation, 
which is critical for boosting efficiency and competitiveness.

### Key Metrics
1.	Total Responses: Out of 504 total responses, 304 were from Anambra State, representing strong regional engagement.
2.	Business Registration Status: Among respondents, 300 businesses are unregistered, and 204 are registered, indicating room for legal and formalization support in the SME sector.

### Findings
From the analysis, several actionable insights emerged, indicating substantial potential for ICT-driven impact:
1.	Business Characteristics: Employee status by year and Belief in ICT Adoption
   - A clustered column chart shows the distribution of employees status by year. The results indicate that many of the businesses have been established for over a year.
   - Registration and Website Presence: Examining the registration status and online presence reveals a low level of formalization, as the majority of businesses are not registered and lack a website.
2.	ICT Adoption Levels: Tool Usage 
  - Usage of ICT Tools: Charting the ICT tools used highlights which are most popular.
  > - Payment tools, such as POS systems and cryptocurrency, are widely used, followed by essential hardware like laptops and CCTV cameras.
3.	Frequency and Purpose of ICT Use
  - Frequency of Internet Use: A pie chart depicting internet usage frequency (daily, weekly, rarely) shows that most businesses integrate ICT into daily operations.
  - Purpose of ICT Use: Analyzing the main purposes for ICT use—such as sales and marketing, communication, and inventory management—reveals that businesses primarily leverage technology to enhance these functions.
4.	Barriers and Concerns in ICT Adoption
   - Concerns with ICT: A bar chart of common concerns, including setup costs, reliance on power supply, and protection against online threats, highlights the primary issues businesses face in adopting ICT.
   - Challenges in Implementation: Key challenges such as securing funding, tracking income, and competing effectively suggest areas where support efforts could benefit businesses.
5.	Future ICT Plans and Needs
  - Plans for New ICT Tools: Visuals on the percentage of businesses planning to adopt new ICT tools show strong interest in innovation.
  - ICT Needs and Support: Charts on requested ICT tools, like continuous internet service, digital sales, procurement tools, and digital marketing resources, emphasize  SME priorities for enhancing ICT use. Commonly requested support types include training and technical assistance.
6.	Impact and Potential of ICT
  - Perceived Value of ICT: Responses show that many business owners believe ICT can address major business challenges, indicating a high level of awareness of ICT’s benefits.
  - Advertising Preferences: Social-media is the primary advertising platform, showcasing a trend towards digital engagement that could benefit from targeted digital marketing tools and support.

### Future Work
SMEs are ready to adopt ICT to drive growth but face challenges like skill gaps, high costs, and limited digital support. Targeted training, affordable solutions, and supportive policies can enhance their efficiency, competitiveness, and reach, with a focus on leveraging social media for advertising. SMEs see the value of ICT, but competing priorities affect their investments. Tailoring ICT strategies to their needs and offering flexible, affordable options can support gradual adoption and improve efficiency and growth.
